The Three Agendas

  • 1. Shift materiality toward a performative model Focus on what things do, not just what they are.
  • 2. Reconnect to critical theory – Bring structuralism, post-structuralism, and humanistic critique into digital media.
  • 3. Apply these ideas to interface design – Design interfaces that show interpretation in action.

What Is Materiality?

Johanna Drucker builds on Matthew Kirschenbaum, who rescued digital studies from the myth of being “immaterial.” He defined:

  • Forensic materiality: tangible evidence: ink, code, fingerprints, or scratches on a disk.
  • Formal materiality: layout, structure, design, and relation between text and image.

Both require interpretation; they are not self-evident.

“Every contact leaves a trace.” – Matthew Kirschenbaum

She adds that we must see materiality not as what things are but as what they do — how they perform within technical, social, and cultural systems.

Performative Materiality

Drucker argues that all texts and systems are event-based, they exist through acts of use, reading, and interpretation. Meaning is not fixed; it is performed each time.

This idea moves us from literal materiality (things as static) to contingent materiality (things as events in flux).

  • Examples: Reading a website differently each time; user paths change meaning.
  • Connections between Steve Krug’s usability and Stanley Fish’s reader-response theory show that reading online is always performative.

Interface as an Event Space

Drucker calls for a new kind of humanistic interface design — one that doesn’t just deliver information, but reveals the act of interpretation.

  • Interfaces are not things but spaces of exchange.
  • They are enunciative systems, they call a subject into being, not just a “user.”
  • Designs should embrace ambiguity, multiplicity, and uncertainty.

By interacting, you create meaning — this is performative materiality in action!

Drucker also discusses distributed materiality (systems interdependence) and systemic ecologies (dynamic networks of matter and meaning).
